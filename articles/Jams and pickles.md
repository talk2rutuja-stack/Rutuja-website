Food is the best expression of my mental state. **See my food to know my mood**. It may sound corny and/or frivolous. Definitely not the most cerebral. It’s personal, emotional and reflective of the chaos (or lack thereof) in the mind. After years of being ashamed of being an emotional eater, I think I am hesitantly ready to own and hone this relationship with food. It feels like claiming an illicit affair - the one that happens at clandestine hours.

On the days of clarity when the brain fog finally lifts, I cook. Spicy curries with ghee tadkas. I find the dabbas of khadaa masala ~ the willingness to go the extra mile evident in cooking. The home smells heavenly, Lata and Hemant Kumar come visiting, or Rahul Deshpande hums O Rangrez into my ears. Memories are made.

And then there are those dull, soggy mornings. Coffee #1 and #2 help lift the spirit but only for a while. Unhealthy quick fixes (or hits) are ordered in. Momentary joys and long-term repentance like yet another toxic relationship.

Motherhood has brought in the in-between. The balance between the extremes of euphoria-utopia and doom-gloom, the mundane. The rigamarole of 3 health-ish meals. Tiffin for opinionated toddlers seem like a challenge on good days, and a deserving punishment on the bad ones. But the mundane helps. Calms the oscillating mind. Finds peace in the boring. Reminder of the golden midway that one looks for in work and life. **The mundane is where muscle memory kicks in, multi-tasking is at peak.** The knots in the mind slowly untie. They may not make dainty bows, and that’s totally fine. The ‘jammed’ clog of negativity starts chipping away while pounding that mirchi cha thecha. The jams melt slowly, a clear mind with creative juices flowing. I hate jams. Limp, sugary and artificial.

Ideas marinate, not for immediate consumption, but for a rainy day later. Generous enough to not expect instant gratification, marinated ideas pickle during the mundane to be stored on a back shelf of the mind. Hoping and waiting. That someday when those soggy mornings return, the pickled fragrance will pull you in, instead of an ordering junkie looking for a quick fix. Almost saying: **Sit down, have that warm phulka with the tangiest kairi-mirchi pickle and you will be alright**.
